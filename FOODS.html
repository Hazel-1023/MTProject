TUPIG

Tupig is a Filipino rice cake made from glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and young coconut strips or shredded coconut, sweetened with sugar, and traditionally grilled after being wrapped in banana leaves. Some variations also include ingredients like peanuts, muscovado sugar, or sesame seeds.

LINAPET

Linapet is an Ilocano delicacy, a type of kakanin (rice cake), made from glutinous rice boiled in sugarcane juice and wrapped in leaves like coconut or banana. It's less sweeter than patupat.

PATUPAT

Patupat is an Ilocano delicacy, a type of kakanin (rice cake), made from glutinous rice boiled in sugarcane juice and wrapped in leaves like coconut or banana. It's more sweeter than linapet

ABUOS

Abuos are the eggs of weaver ants (hantik) and a seasonal delicacy in the Ilocos region of the Philippines, often called "ant caviar". They are collected from nests in trees during the March and April season and are known for their nutty and tangy flavor, and are eaten raw or cooked, commonly fried with garlic, onions, and tomatoes.

TAGAPULOT

Tagapulot is a traditional Ilocano crude molasses or unrefined sugar made from sugarcane juice that is rich in minerals and has a unique, deep flavor. It is used as a natural sweetener in various dishes, such as kakanin (Filipino rice cakes), coffee, and pancakes, and can also be enjoyed on its own as a chewy candy.

BUKTO

Bukto is a common name for a small freshwater or river fish, widely known in the Philippines as a type of goby or mullet. It is a popular food fish, especially in northern regions, and is prepared in various dishes like paksiw (simmered in vinegar) or stewed with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and ginger.
